Just Add Analytics

Posted by Jon Hetrick on May 31, 2020

Google Analytics Test

I have decided to add google analytics to my website that I have been documenting my study of User Experience Science.

Link - User Science Journal

Predictions

Analytics is a crucial part of any business. For this post, I will be first noting some statements that I would think would be true in the analytics of my website. I will then do a review of the little traffic I do have an see if any of those predictions come true.

My blog is about web development, user experience, and accessibility, and I predict to see in my analytics report:

  • Men and women, ages 20-50, from the US.
  • Most users would be coming through a mobile device
  • Users would be either on Chrome or Safari
  • Direct links or Organic search seem to be the most likely approach

Using Google Analytics

Allowing Google Analytics to monitor your traffic is very simple. One will set up an account and use the provided global HTML and put it into each page that you want to track.

Link - Google Analytics

Results

After about 24 hours, these are some of the results I have gathered from Google Analytics:

  • 90% of users came to my site through Direct Link.
  • One user for referral traffic
  • The most popular page was the home page, with 49 views.
  • Most common flow for users was to move from home page > post1 > post 2 > post 3.
  • 9 out 10 users were from the US.
  • All users were using English.
  • 90% of users were using desktop as well mainly using Google Chrome Browser

With the results in, we can see that my initial thoughts were sporadic at best. To start with, I was correct in assuming that most of the users are in the United States. Curiously we had a user from Germany that I found interesting. With the main browser of Google Chrome in the first place, the second place was interestingly Edge / Internet Explorer.

Demographic wise, it looks like I don't have enough information after 24 hours. I Will update if that changes. All the data I could get was nine new users, 9.94 pages of views per session, and an average session length of 9 minutes in 20 seconds.

Overall I was surprised by the amount of detail that Google Analytics has collected. I can see why this would be very beneficial for businesses in making decisions for their company.
